---
title: "The discipline of `skill-sediment`"
date: "2026-04-28"
tags: ["meta", "skills", "process"]
---

Every conversation with Claude Code teaches me something — a sharper way to phrase a prompt, a workaround for a flaky API, a mistake I want it to never make again. But the lesson lives in the conversation, and the conversation ends.

`skill-sediment` is the rule that fixes this: every time I correct Claude in a way that I'd want to apply again, I run `/skill-sediment` and the lesson gets folded into the relevant skill file. Not later. Not in a TODO. Now.

A year of doing this and my skills are unrecognizable from where they started — and Claude makes the same mistakes far less often.
